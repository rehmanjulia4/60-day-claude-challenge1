What is glycemic index?

Glycemic index (GI) tells you how fast a food raises your blood sugar. For diabetes, lower is always better.

≤55
Low GI
Slow, gentle blood sugar rise. Best choice.
56–70
Medium GI
OK in smaller portions. Pair with protein.
71+
High GI
Spikes blood sugar fast. Limit or swap.
Pairing tip: Eating a high-GI food with protein or fat slows the sugar rise. Dates + almonds is a great example. Kabsa rice + grilled fish is better than kabsa rice alone.
💡
Important tips for your health
💊
If you take Metformin: get your B12 checked
Long-term metformin can reduce Vitamin B12 in your blood. Ask your doctor to test it every year. Eat fish, eggs, labneh, and low-fat curd to support B12 naturally.
🥒
Karela (bitter gourd) is your friend
Research supports karela as a natural food that helps manage blood sugar. Add it as a sabzi, stir-fry, or juice. Popular in Indian and some Arabic cooking.
☀
Check your Vitamin D
Very common deficiency in the GCC and South Asia, and it makes diabetes harder to control. Ask your doctor. Food sources: hamour, salmon, fortified milk, eggs.
🚶
Walk after meals
A 10–15 minute walk after eating can reduce blood sugar by 10–30%. This is one of the most effective, simple strategies for T2DM management.
