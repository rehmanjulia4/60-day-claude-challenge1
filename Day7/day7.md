## Reasoning Effort Strategy

**Low:** Formatting, summarizing, rewriting. Speed and structure over depth.

**Standard:** General professional writing, routine communications, initial drafts.

**High:** Strategic planning, research synthesis, resume optimization, presentation development. Benefits from structured reasoning without exhaustive analysis.

**Max:** AI governance frameworks, healthcare transformation strategy, academic writing, board reports, GCC health policy. Work where missing a nuance has real-world consequences.

---

## Cost vs Performance Optimization

**Rule 1:** Default to Sonnet + High Effort for unclassified work.

**Rule 2:** Reserve Opus for work read by peers, published, submitted to boards, or used to advise organizations.

**Rule 3:** Use Haiku aggressively for high-frequency operational tasks.

**Rule 4:** Scale effort with decision reversibility. A LinkedIn post can be edited. A board report submitted to a Ministry of Health cannot.

---

## Biggest Mistakes to Avoid

1. Using one model for everything regardless of task complexity.
2. Under-investing in Opus for governance and policy work where reasoning quality affects outcomes.
3. Overthinking Haiku assignments — speed is the point.
4. Treating reasoning effort as a constant setting rather than a deliberate choice.
5. Not building a repeatable routing system — ad hoc AI usage produces inconsistent results.

---

## Key Learning

Prompt quality matters. Model selection matters first.

The most effective workflow matches task complexity to the appropriate model and reasoning effort. For a healthcare executive producing board-level strategy, peer-reviewed research, governance frameworks, and thought leadership in the same day, this is not a minor optimization. It is the difference between AI assistance and AI excellence.

**Cognitive allocation is a leadership skill.**

---

## Final Recommendation

**Default mode (80% of work):** Sonnet + High Effort

**Strategic mode (governance, board, policy, academic):** Opus + Max Effort

**Speed mode (high-frequency operational tasks):** Haiku + Low Effort
