# Day 2: Prompt Engineering

## What I worked on
- Learned the definition of prompt engineering
- Built a LinkedIn post image comparing weak vs engineered prompts
- Wrote and refined a Day 2 LinkedIn post

## Key learnings
- Prompt engineering is a communication skill, not a technical one
- Specificity drives output quality: audience, format, tone, and goal matter
- The same AI produces completely different results depending on instruction quality

## Prompts I used today
**Weak prompt:** "Write something about marketing."

**Engineered prompt:** "Write a 3-sentence LinkedIn post for a small business owner
explaining why email marketing beats paid ads. Conversational tone. End with a question."

## Observations
## Output Comparison: Weak vs Engineered Prompt

### Explanation Quality
The engineered prompt produced a response that was immediately
usable. The weak prompt ("Write something about marketing")
returned a generic paragraph that could have applied to any
person, any industry, any context. The engineered prompt
produced a specific 3-sentence LinkedIn post written for a
named audience (small business owner), on a named topic
(email vs paid ads), in a named tone (conversational), with
a named structural requirement (end with a question). The
quality difference was not subtle. One needed complete
rewriting. The other was ready to post.

### Structure
The weak prompt output had no structure. It was a block of
text with no headings, no logical flow, and no clear
beginning or end. The engineered prompt output had a defined
shape: an opening stat, a supporting point, and a closing
question. Structure came from the prompt, not from the AI
guessing what I needed.

### Examples
The weak prompt produced no concrete examples. It described
marketing in broad, textbook terms. The engineered prompt
produced a specific claim (email converts better than paid
ads), a specific audience insight (your list is your most
valuable asset), and a specific CTA (are you using it?).
Every line was grounded in something real and actionable.

### Image Design
The weak prompt image was generic, low contrast, and had no
clear visual hierarchy. A viewer could not tell at a glance
what the post was about. The engineered image used a defined
color palette (Claude brown, beige, cream), a two-column
comparison layout, clear section labels, and a visible output
in both panels. The design communicated the concept before
the viewer read a single word. Complexity was controlled.
Clarity was intentional. The visual felt on-brand for a
professional healthcare AI audience.

### Overall Takeaway
The engineered prompt did not just produce better text. It
produced a better thinking process. By forcing me to define
audience, format, tone, and goal before I typed the prompt,
I had already done half the strategic work. The AI completed
the other half cleanly. The weak prompt skipped that thinking
entirely, and the output reflected exactly that.
