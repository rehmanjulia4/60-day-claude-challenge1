Day 6 of the #60DayClaudeChallenge

Today's challenge: AI-powered resume optimization.

Everyone will do the same thing today. Upload a resume, get an ATS score, post a screenshot.

Here is what I learned instead.

I came in with a strong resume. 18+ years of leadership. Multiple graduate degrees. FACHE. Board roles. International executive credentials across the US, UAE, and Saudi Arabia.

The ATS score was 82. After optimization: 96.

But the score was not the lesson.

The lesson was this:

I had three different versions of my resume, each emphasizing a different part of my experience. One led with analytics. One led with association leadership. One led with transformation.

All three were accurate. None of them were fully aligned.

AI did not improve my resume by adding new experience.

It showed me that I was diluting my own executive brand by telling three stories instead of one.

Before:
Analytics and Informatics Leader

After:
Healthcare Executive. Transformation and Governance Leader. VP and C-Suite candidate across GCC and US markets.

No new achievements.
No invented metrics.
Just one clear story.

The biggest shift was moving my board and governance roles (ACHE VP MENA, ACHDM International Chair, Because Organization Board, DIGIHEALTH Lab Advisory Board) out of body text and into a dedicated section, searchable by executive search firms and ATS systems.

Executive search firms use the same keyword-matching logic as ATS systems. If your governance credentials are buried in paragraph text, they are invisible.

One question worth asking yourself:

If a recruiter at Korn Ferry or Spencer Stuart read your resume in 30 seconds, would they immediately know who you are as an executive?

Not what you have done. Who you are.

That is the real test.
